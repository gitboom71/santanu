## A Quick Overview

**A family floater** is a health insurance plan that extends the coverage to the entire family rather than just an individual. A floater plan brings all the members of the family under an umbrella cover, allowing each member to access benefits from a larger common pool. Moreover, such policies also provide convenience to policyholders by allowing them to get coverage for each family member for a single premium.

 
 ## How it Works
 **Shared Coverage:** Instead of each person having their own limit, the entire sum insured is available for any covered family member. For example, if you have a ₹10 Lakh floater policy, any single member can use the full amount, or it can be divided among members who need medical care during the year.
 
 **Members Included:** You can typically cover yourself, your legally wedded spouse, dependent children (usually up to age 25 or 30), and sometimes dependent parents or in-laws.
 
 **Premium Pricing:** The premium is generally determined by the age of the oldest member being insured.

 ## Benefits of a Family Floater Plan
 
 **Affordability:**  One annual premium covers the whole household, making it more budget-friendly.

 **Flexibility:** Since it is highly unlikely that all family members will be hospitalized simultaneously, a floater provides higher, shared coverage at a lower cost than individual plans.
 
 **Tax Savings:** Premiums paid for family floater policies qualify for tax deductions under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act.

### What is Not So Good About Family Floater Plans?

* **The obvious turn-off in a floater** is that in case of more than one claim within an year, the member making the subsequent claim has to do away with a thinned out cover (left out of the amount realised for the first claim).
  
•	Most of the floater plans available in the market cover only the policyholder, their spouse and children, while excluding parents and siblings.

•	A family floater plan can only be renewed till the oldest member reaches the maximum renewability age.


## When to Choose Individual vs. Floater

**Choose a Floater if:**   You are a young nuclear family (e.g., a couple with young children) where everyone is generally healthy and low-risk.

**Families Looking for Affordable Options** - Since the premium for family floater health insurance is lower than the combined premium for separate individual policies for each member, people who want cost-effective coverage can opt for family floater plans.

**Families with Similar Aged Young Members** - The premium for a family floater plan is calculated based on the age of the oldest member. With similar aged young members, you will also not have to pay a higher premium due to the age gap. Even the health risk levels are also similar in such a group, hence sharing sum insured does not become risky.

### **Choose Individual Plans if:** 

You are covering elderly parents or members with chronic illnesses, or if your family size is large. In these cases, individual coverage guarantees that one member’s medical expenses will not deplete the healthcare funds for the rest of the family.
